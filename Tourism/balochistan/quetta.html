Welcome to Quetta!

Quetta, the capital city of Balochistan province in Pakistan, is a city of rich cultural heritage, scenic landscapes, and warm hospitality. Nestled in the embrace of rugged mountains, Quetta offers a unique blend of natural beauty, historical landmarks, and vibrant bazaars.

Key Features:

  • Hanna Lake: Visit the picturesque Hanna Lake, a tranquil oasis surrounded by hills, offering boating facilities and a serene environment for picnics.
  • Ziarat Valley: Explore the scenic Ziarat Valley, known for its pleasant weather, lush greenery, and the famous Juniper Forest, one of the oldest and largest juniper forests in the world.
  • Quaid-e-Azam Residency: Discover the historic Quaid-e-Azam Residency, a well-preserved building where the founder of Pakistan, Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah, spent his last days.
  • Bolan Pass: Experience the awe-inspiring beauty of the Bolan Pass, a mountainous gorge connecting Quetta with the rest of the country, known for its rugged terrain and scenic views.
  • Shopping and Bazaars: Explore the bustling bazaars of Quetta, such as Liaquat Bazaar and Kandahari Bazaar, and indulge in traditional handicrafts, spices, and colorful Balochi fabrics.
  • Local Cuisine: Sample the delicious Balochi cuisine, including the famous Sajji and Kaak, and experience the flavors of the region.
